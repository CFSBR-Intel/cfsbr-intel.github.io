About our domain setup (for the Scholar team's reference):
CFSBR maintains three production domains that are intentionally co-primary. cfsbr-intel.github.io is the GitHub Pages mirror registered against the Crossref DOI prefix and serves as the official landing-page URL for each registered article (this is the URL the DOI resolves to). cfsbr.gmrafi.com.bd is the Bangladesh/regional publisher website (Vercel-hosted) used as the publisher and imprint URL. cfsbr.com is the new global English-language brand domain (canonical short address for international traffic), registered and pointed at the same publication infrastructure. All three serve identical, world-readable content. Each article page therefore carries canonical and citation tags pointing to the GitHub Pages article URL, while the JSON-LD publisher block and the imprint pages point to the branded domains. This tri-domain setup is fully reflected in sameAs and our sitemap.

- DOIs are registered with Crossref (DataCite member prefix 10.67226) and resolve via https://doi.org/ to the article's GitHub Pages landing page.

4. Editorial and Publishing Process
- Submissions are received through a public Call for Papers page (https://cfsbr.gmrafi.com.bd/publications/call-for-institutional-papers, mirrored at https://cfsbr-intel.github.io/publications/call-for-institutional-papers).
- Each accepted paper is assigned an internal serial and DOI suffix on a rolling basis by the CFSBR editorial desk.
- Editorial review (scope, methodology, citation integrity) is performed before DOI registration and Crossref deposit.
- Articles are versioned (for example v1, v2) and changes are tracked through DOI metadata deposits.

5. Open-Access and Permanence
All articles in the series are released under CC BY-NC 4.0 (free to read, share, and cite with attribution; non-commercial reuse). Full-text PDFs and landing pages are intended to remain permanently accessible at their canonical URLs, with the publisher's commitment to long-term preservation of the institutional repository. The GitHub Pages mirror guarantees a stable, third-party-hosted, open-science-grade copy; the branded domain is the public-facing imprint.
