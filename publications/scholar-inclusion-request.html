Prepared: June 2026 · Publisher: Centre for Fintech and Strategic Business Research (CFSBR) · Document status: ready to send
About our domain setup (for the Scholar team's reference): CFSBR maintains two production domains that are intentionally co-primary. cfsbr-intel.github.io is the GitHub Pages mirror registered against the Crossref DOI prefix and serves as the official landing-page URL for each registered article (this is the URL the DOI resolves to). cfsbr.gmrafi.com.bd is the branded publisher website (Vercel-hosted) used as the publisher and imprint URL. Both serve identical, world-readable content. Each article page therefore carries canonical and citation tags pointing to the GitHub Pages article URL, while the JSON-LD publisher block and the imprint pages point to the branded domain. This dual-domain setup is fully reflected in sameAs and our sitemap.

3. Technical and Metadata Readiness

  • Every article landing page exposes Google Scholar citation_* tags (title, author, author_institution, publisher, publication_date, doi, technical_report_number, abstract, keywords, pdf_url, abstract_html_url, fulltext_world_readable).
  • Dublin Core DC.* equivalents are present for cross-crawler compatibility.
  • Open Graph and Twitter Card tags are present for social and aggregator crawlers.
  • Schema.org ScholarlyArticle JSON-LD is present on each article page, including publisher, identifier, doi, license, sameAs, and BreadcrumbList.
  • Canonical URL is declared in <link rel="canonical"> on the GitHub Pages mirror (the URL the DOI resolves to).
  • The branded domain (cfsbr.gmrafi.com.bd) is referenced from sameAs, the publisher.url, the publisher.logo.url, and the breadcrumb list, so Scholar can crawl it for imprint and editorial context.
  • robots.txt permits all user-agents; a valid sitemap.xml is published at https://cfsbr.gmrafi.com.bd/sitemap.xml and mirrored at https://cfsbr-intel.github.io/sitemap.xml.
  • Full-text PDFs are real PDFs (not scanned images): they contain a live text layer that is indexable by Scholar's PDF crawler.
  • DOIs are registered with Crossref (DataCite member prefix 10.67226) and resolve via https://doi.org/ to the article's GitHub Pages landing page.

4. Editorial and Publishing Process

  • Submissions are received through a public Call for Papers page (https://cfsbr.gmrafi.com.bd/publications/call-for-institutional-papers, mirrored at https://cfsbr-intel.github.io/publications/call-for-institutional-papers).
  • Each accepted paper is assigned an internal serial and DOI suffix on a rolling basis by the CFSBR editorial desk.
  • Editorial review (scope, methodology, citation integrity) is performed before DOI registration and Crossref deposit.
  • Articles are versioned (for example v1, v2) and changes are tracked through DOI metadata deposits.

5. Open-Access and Permanence

All articles in the series are released under CC BY-NC 4.0 (free to read, share, and cite with attribution; non-commercial reuse). Full-text PDFs and landing pages are intended to remain permanently accessible at their canonical URLs, with the publisher's commitment to long-term preservation of the institutional repository. The GitHub Pages mirror guarantees a stable, third-party-hosted, open-science-grade copy; the branded domain is the public-facing imprint.
